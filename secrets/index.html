Secrets

Lord and Lady Peacock:

"The Hines couple uncovered a scandal involving my hidden military past and the covert operations I conducted during wartime. I couldn’t let that come to light—it would have ruined everything I’ve worked for. Lady Peacock, always one step ahead, helped me craft an alibi while spreading just enough misinformation to keep suspicion away from us. It wasn’t easy, but we had no choice."

Mr. and Mrs. Green:

"The Hines couple were digging into my business records and found something they shouldn’t have—financial irregularities that could have destroyed me. I tried to reason with them, but they wouldn’t back down. Mrs. Green, as resourceful as ever, suggested we take matters into our own hands to protect everything we’ve built. It wasn’t something we wanted to do, but they left us no choice."

Miss and Mr. Scarlet:

"The Hines couple humiliated me in public, criticizing my work and tarnishing my reputation. They had no idea how much that hurt, how much it threatened my career. Mr. Scarlet saw my pain and, inspired by one of his stories, suggested a way to stop them from ever speaking ill of me again. We knew it was risky, but the idea of letting them ruin me was unbearable."

Madam and Missy Mustard:

"The Hines couple were digging into my international ventures, accusing me of smuggling artifacts. I tried to brush it off, but Missy overheard their plans to report me to the authorities. She came to me with a solution I hadn’t dared to consider. Together, we decided we couldn’t let them destroy everything we’ve worked for, no matter the cost."

Mrs. and Dr. White:

"The Hines couple were relentless, asking questions about the tragic accident that happened at my clinic. They wouldn’t let it go, and I knew what they were digging for could ruin me. Mrs. White saw how desperate I was and insisted we act before it was too late. It wasn’t something I ever wanted to do, but we had to protect ourselves."

Professor and Lady Plum:

"They saw too much—one of my experiments, something I hadn’t perfected yet. The Hines couple didn’t understand the significance of my work, and they threatened to expose me. Lady Plum, always thinking ahead, came up with a plan. We didn’t want to hurt anyone, but they left us no choice if we wanted to preserve our future."

Inspector and Madam Noir:

"They knew. Somehow, the Hines couple had found out about the evidence I tampered with in that old case. It would have ended my career, my life’s work. Madam Noir suggested we use the gathering as an opportunity to silence them permanently. I didn’t want to do it, but the risk was too great. We had to act."

Mr. and Madam Mahogany:

"They called me a fraud. The Hines couple discovered that some of the artifacts I sold them weren’t authentic, and they demanded restitution. Madam Mahogany reminded me that we couldn’t let them ruin us—our reputation, our livelihood. It wasn’t easy, but we did what we had to do to keep our secrets safe."

Dr. and Professor Orchid:

"It was my fault. One of my experiments had unintended consequences, and a guest paid the price. The Hines couple started investigating, getting too close to the truth. Professor Orchid and I knew what would happen if they exposed us. We couldn’t let that happen, so we took steps to protect ourselves. It was the only way."
